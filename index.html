My research interests lie largely in number theory, especially arithmetic geometry and the Langlands program. I’m particularly drawn to p-adic and global Galois representations and to arithmetic local-to-global principles, where number theory, algebraic geometry, representation theory, and analysis intersect in beautiful ways.

Outside of mathematics, I enjoy writing fiction and philosophy, over-sharing elliptic curves, finite fields, p-adic numbers, and number theory with unsuspecting family and friends, playing 1|0 chess (or possibly 3|2), and pursuing independent cryptographic projects.

Research

  • MSc Thesis (Imperial College London) — In progress, 2025. Conducted under the supervision of George Boxer, focusing on p-adic Hodge theory and modular forms.
  • On Deformation of Perfectoid Purity in Gorenstein Domains — with Baily, Dovgodko, and Simpson.
    We investigate when perfectoid purity, a mixed-characteristic analog of F-purity, deforms from a quotient \( R/pR \) to a complete Gorenstein local ring \( R \). This work establishes a connection between vanishing of local cohomology in absolute integral closures and deformation of splinters. [arXiv:2504.02966]
  • Some Applications of the Brenner–Monsky Quartic — with Dovgodko and Simpson. In preparation, 2025.
    We construct a two-parameter family of Segre products involving Monsky’s quartics with varying Hilbert–Kunz multiplicities and present a new counterexample to localization of tight closure in the mildly singular case. [Early Draft]
  • Examples of Lie Algebras with Specified Newton Polygons — with Alwan, K. Huang, T. Huang, and Stovall. In preparation, 2025.
    We determine all minimal Newton polygons on \( \mathbb{R}^n \) for \( n \leq 13 \), and provide bounds for Newton polygons in arbitrary dimensions.

Other Research & Projects

  • The Rising Sea Solutions — A self-contained write-up of exercises and results from Vakil’s The Rising Sea. [PDF] [GitHub]
  • The Arithmetic of Elliptic Curves Solutions — A collection of expanded proofs, remarks, and exercise solutions from Silverman’s The Arithmetic of Elliptic Curves. [PDF] [GitHub]
  • Describing a Hyperbolic Surface: From Lengths and Twists to Matrices — UW–Madison MxM Undergraduate Research Seminar, Fall 2022.
    We constructed explicit generators in \( \mathrm{PSL}(2, \mathbb{R}) \) for Fuchsian groups representing hyperbolic surfaces (e.g. pairs of pants), using twist and length parameters. [program link]
  • A Proof of Quadratic Reciprocity by Galois Theory — A complete Galois-theoretic proof of quadratic reciprocity, expanding on a sketch by Prof. Ana Caraiani. [PDF] [GitHub]
  • Segre & Veronese Algorithms in Macaulay2 — Macaulay2 code for computing presentations of Veronese subrings and Segre products of graded rings. [GitHub (Segre)] [GitHub (Veronese)]
  • Original Number Theory Problems — A collection of 9 self-authored problems with complete solutions exploring Diophantine equations and modular arithmetic; one was used for a UW–Madison Putnam Club meeting, where students attempted the problem and I presented the solution. [PDF] [GitHub]
